Professional Identity

Fashion, structure, and creative development.

Urwa Farhan is a fashion and design graduate whose work explores concept-led fashion, structured garment construction, styling, and visual presentation. Her creative practice combines research, sketch exploration, pattern development, and final garment construction to transform ideas into wearable forms.

About

Emerging fashion and design professional.

I am a fashion and design graduate with a Pearson BTEC Level 5 Higher National Diploma in Art and Design Fashion, currently progressing through my top-up studies. My background combines creative design training with professional internship experience, allowing me to approach fashion with both artistic sensitivity and practical understanding.

My experience includes pattern cutting, garment making, styling, trend forecasting, CAD, visual presentation, and design development. I am particularly interested in concept-led fashion design, structured garment construction, and understanding how global trends and international standards influence the fashion industry.

My creative practice explores the relationship between structure, fabric, silhouette, and the human body. I enjoy developing ideas through research, sketch exploration, pattern work, and garment construction, allowing concepts to progress from initial inspiration to final outcomes.

Design Process

My Creative Process

My design process moves from research and experimentation into technical development and final garment construction.

01

Concept Research

Each project begins with visual and conceptual research. Moodboards, references, textures, and themes help define the design direction.

02

Sketch Exploration

Multiple sketches are produced to explore silhouettes, proportions, garment details, and the overall visual language of the project.

03

Pattern Development

Patterns translate the design concept into a structured three-dimensional form, allowing the garment to move from idea to construction.

04

Toile Development

Prototype garments are used to test fit, proportion, structure, and silhouette before final fabric and finishing decisions are made.

05

Final Garment Construction

The final garment brings together concept, fabric, pattern, construction, and styling to create a completed fashion outcome.
